Problem 1. Optimal stopping

Description. Optimal stopping is concerned with the problem of choosing a time to take a particular action, in order to maximise an expected reward or minimise an expected cost. It other words it helps us to make decision when we should stop searching and choose current option.
Example: Secretary Problem. We want to hire new secretary. We received a lot of CVs. But we don't have time to do interview with all of them. So question is: who should we hire? Where should we stop searching and hire current candidate?
Solution: 37%. Interview 37% of candidates. And then choose next who is better then anyone from these 37%.

Problem 2. Explore-exploit tradeoff

Description. The exploration, exploitation trade-off is a dilemma we frequently face in choosing between options. Should you choose what you know and get something close to what you expect ('exploit') or choose something you aren`t sure about and possibly learn more ('explore')?
Example: Restaurant Problem. You want to go to a restaurant. But where should you go? Should you go to the restaurant which you've already known and which is good? Or should you go to new restaurant and try something new risking bad experience?
Solution: Time. If you plan to stay in the town longer then it's better to choose a new restaurant. Information which you will gather can help you make better decision in the future. But if you plan to stay in the town only for short time then it's better to go to the restaurant which you've already known and which is good. Just explore something what is good.

Problem 3. Replacement problem

Description. We have some storage with limited space and few items inside. When we get new item and we have to store it then which item should we replace in storage?
Example: Wardrobe. We have a wardrobe full of clothes. And we buy new cloth. Which cloth in a wardrobe should we replace?
Solution: The longest not used cloth. We should replace this cloth which has not been used by the longest time.
