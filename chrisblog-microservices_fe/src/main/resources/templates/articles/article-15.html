Genome

Genome is the collection of all genes in the body. Human's body has about 25 000 genes. The main purpose of genes is to produce proteins. Genes can be divided into:
  • Coding Sequence - 3%: these genes are responsible for creating proteins. There are very similar in the most of organisms. For instance human and mouse Coding Sequence is the same in 99%;
  • Regulation Sequence - 97%: determine what, when and how many proteins should be created by Coding Sequence.
The emergence object is an object which can be changed by environment. For instance water can have following forms:
  • Ice: environment temperature below 0 Celsius degrees;
  • Water: environment temperature between 0 and 100 Celsius degrees;
  • Steam: environemtn temperature above 100 Celsius degrees.
Genome is also such emergence object. It means that genome is changed by environment. In other words genome is not one constant piece of music. It's rather music instrument. Environment can play different pieces of music on it.
Example 1: Mouses DBA hates drugs. Mouses C57 loves drugs. But after a long starvation their genome changes. Mouses DBA starts to love drugs. Mouses C57 starts to hate drugs.
Example 2: Mouses DBA hates drugs. Mouses C57 loves drugs. Italian's mother" is very affectionate. Spartan's mother is not affectionate. Mouse DBA brought up by Italian's mother loves drugs more. Mouse DBA brought up by Spartan's mother loves drugs is on the same level. Mouse C57 brought up by Italian's mother hates drugs more. Mouse C57 brought up by Spartan's mother hates drugs is on the same level.
Environment can change genome in following way:
  • Genetic Selection;
  • Epigenetic Selection;
  • Transcription.
Genetic Selection is the best known type of selections discovered by Karol Darwin. This selection is very long - takes hundreds or thousands of years. This selection can be summarized with following sentence: biology proposes but the environment decides. In other words: Genetic Selection is our instrument with 25 000 strings.
Epigenetic Selection is much faster then Genetic Selection. Here genome can be changed during one or two generations. Goal of this selection is to adjust to rapid changes of environment like drought or cooling. Example: one woman was born on Sicily. She was very close to mother. But she moved to Paris for studing in medical school. And new environment changed her. She wasn't so close with mother. She focused on medicine. She got married and had child. But after born of the child she changed again. She focused only on the baby. She dropped medicine. She started to be very close to her mother agein. As her husband said: she started to be completely different person. In other words: Epigenetic Selection is change in our instrument with 25 000 strings.
Transcription Factors changes our genome in the fastests way. It determines what, when and how many proteins should be produced in our body. Steps:
  • Factor's Convertion: physical factors (what we see, hear, feel etc.) are converted into chemical factors;
  • Factor's Transportation: chemical factors are transported to synapses in a brain;
  • Create Transcription Factors: synapses converts chemical factors into transcription factors;
  • Create Proteins: transcription factors go to cells nuclei and decide what, when and how many proteins should be produced.
In other words: Transcription Factors are fingers which play melody on our instrument with 25 000 strings.

Happiness vs Pleasure

Happiness and pleasure are strictly connected with resources which are necessary to live. We need three types of resources:
  • Air: most available. We don't stock it. We are dead after 3 minutes of lack of air;
  • Water: less available but still there is a lot of water on the earth. We stock it a little. We are dead after 3 days of lack of water;
  • Food: the least available. We store the greatest amount of it. We are dead after 40 days of lack of food.
Happiness and pleasure are related to the availability of resources and their storage:
  • Happiness: is connected with all resources: air, water and food. We feel it when we compensate for the shortages. For instance we feel happiness when we are thirsty and we dring glass of water;
  • Pleasure: is connected only with one resource: food. We feel it when we eat more then we need just to stock a food for the future. For instance we feel pleasure when we full after dinner but we eat additional dessert.
But about 50 000 years ago human discovered art. From this moment happiness and pleasure are not only connected with biological resources. Happiness and pleasure became connected with such abstract things like beauty, order etc. We started not to owe survival to pleasure and happiness. We started to live to feel happiness and pleasure.

Mental Disorders

Types of mental disorders:
  • Neurological: the brain is not doing enough or well enough of what it is supposed to do. Examples: Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease etc.;
  • Pathologies: the brain does too much of what it should be doing. Examples: ADHD etc.
Mental illness is determined by three factors:
  • Rational Cause: whether the behavior is proportionate and appropriate to the situation;
  • Frequency: how common is the behavior;
  • Nuisance: how much this behavior interferes with life.

The Obesity

The obesity is the result of our natural behavior. When we see something sweet or salty, we have a natural tendency to eat it excessively. But now the environment has changed and this natural behavior is becoming a problem.
Ways to fight obesity:
  • What?: eat less palatable foods that contain less sugar, salt and fats. Keep your food out of sight;
  • How?: meal times should be constant;
  • With who?: eat alone because eating in a group makes us eat more.

Drug Addiction

There are the following types of drugs:
  • Tobacco Derivatives: for instance tobacco. Addiction: 33%. They simulate neurotransmitters by affecting the receptors, but more intensively;
  • Opioids: for instance heroin or morphine. Addiction: 20%. They simulate neurotransmitters by affecting the receptors, but more intensively;
  • Cannabinoids: for instance marijuana. Addiction: 10%. They simulate neurotransmitters by affecting the receptors, but more intensively;
  • Alcoholic Drinks: for instance alcohol. Addiction: 20%. They intensify the action of neurotransmitters by increasing the number of signals or the sensitivity of receptors;
  • Psychostimulants: for instance cocaine and amphetamines. Addiction: 20%. They intensify the action of neurotransmitters by increasing the number of signals or the sensitivity of receptors.
The legitimacy of drugs does not depend on the degree of addiction but on the impact on society:
  • Tabaco: none;
  • Alcohol: there is but a large dose. It's easy to dose;
  • Marijuana: reduces social adaptation. It is bad for young people because it hinders remembering and motivation. Not so bad for adults;
  • Cocaine: a person begins to think that everything is allowed to him;
  • Heroin: great pleasure so a person stops doing anything else.
Drugs cause an increase in dopamine. This increase may be:
  • Natural: it is not large and drops after the stimulus has ceased;
  • Artificial: it is large and remains after the stimulus has ceased. It increases when the stimulant is taken again.
Addiction stages:
  • Occasional use
  • Intensive and constant use
  • pathological use
Pathological use is when the substance-acquisition behavior is:
  • Invasive: more and more drugs, less and less other things;
  • Unrestrained: this action cannot be stopped.
The susceptibility of the individual leads to addiction. Some are less susceptible and some are more susceptible. Factors:
  • Overactive dopamine-forming system: such an individual feels the pleasure much more. It is either caused by genes or by the environment;
  • Underdevelopment of the prefrontal cortex: drugs make everyone lose their ability to learn. Later most regain it but not addicted. They cannot change their behavior. Their behavior crystallizes.
Drug addiction is a disease and should be treated as such. Solutions:
  • Large research expenditure
  • Heal people early on
  • Legalize (but only when we can heal)

Tumors

A tumor is an abnormal growth of body tissue. The causes of tumors may be
  • Food;
  • Sun;
  • Cigarettes;
  • Viruses.
There have to be three mutations in a cell for cancer to develop:
  • In a protooncogen: the cell starts dividing without permission. It is as if the accelerator pedal in the car is stuck;
  • In the suppressor gene: this gene is responsible for controlling division. It's as if the car's brake broke;
  • Increased production of telomerase: the amount of telomerase determines the lifespan of a given cell. It's as if an old car is being repaired over and over again.

Proteins

Proteins are the building blocks of our body. All proteins have the same structure based on a chain of 22 amino acids. The differences between proteins are the order of the amino acids and this is what determines the DNA.
Types of proteins:
  • Structural: determine the shape of the body;
  • Enzymes: determine what the body can do.
Protein types according to their location in the cell:
  • Membrane proteins: surround the cell;
  • Cytoplasm proteins: fill the cell;
  • Cell nucleus proteins: fill the cell nucleus;
  • Mitochondrial proteins: they create energy.

Hormones

Hormones are used to communicate with cells. These are so-called mediators. They modify the activity of specific receptors in the recipient cell. Receptors are proteins located on the cell.
The action of hormones:
  • The key enters the lock: due to its specific shape the hormone binds to the appropriate receptor;
  • The key rotates in the lock: the receptor changes form;.
  • The door opens: the cell changes activity.

Chloroplasts and mitochondria

Chloroplasts are organelles made of bacteria that are found in plant cells. They convert solar energy into glucose.
Mitochondria are organelles made of bacteria that are found in animal cells. They convert glucose into energy - ATP which is a universal fuel.
Chloroplasts and mitochondria were common bacteria in the past. Over time, however, they became part of plant and animal cells. In other words, converting solar energy into sugar and sugar into solar energy would not be possible without bacteria.
